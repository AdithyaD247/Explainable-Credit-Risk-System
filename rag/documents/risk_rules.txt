Risk Classification Rules

Low Risk:

* Predicted default probability below 20 percent.
* Strong external credit scores.
* Stable employment and income.

Medium Risk:

* Predicted default probability between 20 percent and 50 percent.
* Moderate debt burden.
* Some financial risk indicators present.

High Risk:

* Predicted default probability above 50 percent.
* Weak external credit scores.
* High debt-to-income ratio.
* Limited employment history.

Additional Risk Indicators:

* High credit-to-income ratio.
* Short employment duration.
* Multiple prior defaults.
* Low external credit score values.
